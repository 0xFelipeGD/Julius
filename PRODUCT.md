# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Julius is for individual users tracking personal expenses from a phone-sized web app. They are usually acting quickly: recording a spend, checking what the month looks like, confirming a subscription or fixed monthly cost, or exporting a statement.

Felipe also uses the product as owner/admin and needs a restricted administration surface for user cleanup.

## Product Purpose

Julius helps a user keep a clean monthly picture of spending by combining text-based chat capture, user-owned categories, dashboards, statements, subscriptions, and fixed monthly costs. Julius 2.0 narrows the product to English, EUR, and one assistant identity so the app feels focused and predictable.

Success means existing user data survives the upgrade, new spending can be recorded with fewer choices, recurring money is visible before it is paid, and the mobile app feels trustworthy enough to live on a phone home screen.

## Brand Personality

Clear, financially aware, and lightly opinionated. Julius should feel practical and calm, not jokey, corporate, or over-decorated.

Julius can have character in chat copy, but the app interface itself should stay quiet, direct, and task-focused.

## Anti-references

- Generic dark navy finance dashboards.
- AI-looking purple/blue gradients and glass cards.
- Cartoon mascot UI or multi-persona gimmicks.
- Heavy bank-enterprise styling with dense tables and cold copy.
- Marketing landing-page composition inside the authenticated app.
- Receipt scanner, budget-limit, or region-selector surfaces from Julius 1.x.

## Design Principles

1. Keep the task visible: every screen should make the next useful financial action obvious.
2. Preserve trust: never hide data changes, destructive actions, or generated transactions.
3. Make recurring money visible: expected monthly costs deserve first-class treatment, not settings-level placement.
4. Prefer calm density: compact enough for repeated mobile use, but with enough spacing to avoid dashboard noise.
5. Use one coherent product voice: English, EUR, Julius only.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls. Touch targets should be at least 44px where practical. All controls need visible focus states, keyboard-accessible semantics, and non-color-only state communication. Motion should be short, state-driven, and respectful of reduced-motion preferences.
